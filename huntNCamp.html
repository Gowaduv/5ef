Geoff Keyes

Journeyman Bladesmith

  • 2013 Camp Knife | $500
    This is one of my favorite blade shapes combined with just about my favorite handle material. Osage orange is a wood from the US mid-West. Where it is native it is regarded as a weed and is used for fence posts. It is one of the most durable woods around, and is the premier bow wood in the US. It is tough to work, but yields a silky surface and a lovely color.
    OL: 15 1/2"; BL: 10 1/4"; Guard: Nickel Steel; Blade: 1084; Handle: Bone and fiber spacers, domed NS pins; No sheath.
  • 2013 Fancy Hunter | $375
    This is a small, fairly ornate, hatchet point hunter. The guard,one of the ebony spacers, and the underside of the handle are textured, the rest of the handle is NS, ebony, and ironwood.
    OL: 10 1/2"; BL: 5 7/8th"; Handle: Ebony, Nickel Silver, Ironwood; No sheath.
